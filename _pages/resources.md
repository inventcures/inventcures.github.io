A curated collection of papers, articles, and books that have shaped my thinking about cancer research, drug discovery, and rare diseases.

## Cancer Research Papers

Papers that provide deep insights into cancer biology and treatment:

1. **[Why do patients with cancer die](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41568-024-00708-4){:target="_blank"}**
   A fascinating exploration of the mechanisms behind cancer mortality.

2. **[From patterns to patients: Advances in clinical machine learning for cancer diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0092867423000946){:target="_blank"}**
   Comprehensive overview of ML applications in oncology.

## Drug Discovery & Clinical Trials

Understanding the complex landscape of bringing new therapies to patients:

1. **[Global Oncology Trends 2024 by IQVIA](https://www.iqvia.com/insights/the-iqvia-institute/reports-and-publications/reports/global-oncology-trends-2024){:target="_blank"}**
   Annual industry report on the state of oncology drug development.

2. **[A target map of clinical combination therapies in oncology](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10441974/){:target="_blank"}**
   Analysis of combination therapy strategies from clinicaltrials.gov.

## Deep Dives into Drug Discovery

Excellent long-form pieces, mostly by Alex Telford and Asimov Press:

1. **[The pharma industry from Paul Janssen to today](https://atelfo.github.io/2023/12/23/biopharma-from-janssen-to-today.html){:target="_blank"}**
   Why drugs got harder to develop and what we can do about it.

2. **[Some questions about biotech that I find interesting](https://atelfo.github.io/2024/04/01/biotech-questions.html){:target="_blank"}**
   Thoughtful exploration of open problems in biotech.

3. **[Intelligence as efficient model building](https://atelfo.github.io/2023/05/17/intelligence-as-efficient-model-building.html){:target="_blank"}**
   On the nature of intelligence and its applications.

4. **[The origins of the lab mouse](https://press.asimov.com/articles/mouse-microscope){:target="_blank"}** (Asimov Press)
   A fascinating history piece. If you enjoy this, subscribe to Asimov Press and follow [Niko McCarty](https://x.com/NikoMcCarty).

5. **[Why I should have loved biology](https://jsomers.net/i-should-have-loved-biology/){:target="_blank"}**
   A beautiful essay on biology education.

### Inspiring Parent Stories

1. **[Hunting down my son's killer](https://matt.might.net/articles/my-sons-killer/){:target="_blank"}** by Matt Might
   One of my all-time favorite reads. Matt, a CS professor, chronicles the diagnostic odyssey for his son Bertrand, and how he and his wife dropped everything to find a therapy. *[Bertrand passed away in 2020. Here is [Matt's reflection on Bertrand's life](https://bertrand.might.net/articles/echoes-of-bertrand/)]*

   More from Matt:
   - [Accepting the unacceptable](https://bertrand.might.net/articles/accepting-the-unacceptable/){:target="_blank"}
   - [The algorithm for precision medicine](https://bertrand.might.net/articles/algorithm-for-precision-medicine/){:target="_blank"}

2. **Saving Lydia** by Rohan & Jen Seth
   Another inspiring story of parents fighting for their daughter.
   - [Why I'm Open Sourcing My Baby](https://medium.com/lydian-accelerator/saving-lydia-62a1c0bdf0fb){:target="_blank"}
   - [A Path from Diagnosis to Treatment in 3 months](https://medium.com/lydian-accelerator/saving-lydia-a-path-from-diagnosis-to-treatment-in-3-months-de9a1d8f0b95){:target="_blank"}
   - [Research Strategy Document](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1SSAx3OI3AX1SePqt8hoyU6CsSzxY3UmewUgwezcw-3Y/edit?usp=sharing){:target="_blank"}

3. **[A Prion Love Story](https://www.newyorker.com/books/page-turner/a-prion-love-story){:target="_blank"}** (The New Yorker)
   Eric & Sonia Vallabh, two non-scientists, become researchers after Sonia tests positive for the genetic mutation that killed her mother. *[Also in Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-married-researchers-racing-to-stop-prion-disease/)*

## Recommended Books

### On Rare Diseases

- **[Breath From Salt](https://www.gatesnotes.com/Breath-From-Salt){:target="_blank"}** by Bijal P. Trivedi
  The definitive account of the CF drug discovery journey. At 615 pages, it's a commitment, but provides a ringside view of decades of effort. *[Goodreads reviews](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/53847362-breath-from-salt)*

- **[Chasing My Cure](https://chasingmycure.com/about/){:target="_blank"}** by David Fajgenbaum
  A physician-scientist's journey to find a treatment for his own rare disease.

- **[We the Scientists](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/537125/we-the-scientists-by-amy-dockser-marcus/){:target="_blank"}** by Amy Dockser Marcus
  Pulitzer Prize-winning WSJ reporter on patient-led research.
